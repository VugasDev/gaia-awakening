# Power Up

**Tier 3 — Chapter 1 of 5**

Create and Mekanism speak different power languages — Stress Units and Forge Energy (FE) respectively. This chapter uses Create Crafts and Additions to bridge them, then adds Powah generators and XNet cabling to build a unified power and logistics backbone for everything that follows.

Requires: `t2_create_brass` quest 6 (Precision Mechanism) and `t2_mekanism` quest 6 (Stored Energy) to begin.

---

## Main-Path Quests

| # | Quest | Aim |
|---|-------|-----|
| 1 | **Rotation Becomes Power** | Build a Create Alternator to convert Create shaft rotation into Forge Energy. |
| 2 | **Electric Motor** | Add a Create Electric Motor to convert FE back into rotation — enabling energy-routed machine power. |
| 3 | **Powah Unlocked** | Craft a Basic Furnator from Powah for a dedicated, scalable fuel-burning FE generator. |
| 4 | **Energy Storage** | Build a Powah Basic Battery to buffer power and prevent machine starvation during demand spikes. |
| 5 | **XNet — The Nervous System** | Build an XNet Controller with 4 connectors to route items, fluids and energy through one cable type. |

## Tips

- Place your **Alternator** on a high-stress shaft (windmill or large water wheel setup) before connecting Mekanism machines — they consume FE continuously.
- **XNet** (quest 5) has a learning curve but it pays off massively: one controller can manage your entire base's item routing, fluid piping, and energy distribution through color-coded connectors.
- Powah generators upgrade through six tiers. Start with a Basic Furnator (quest 3) and upgrade it as you progress — do not build an army of Basic units.
- **Wind Generators** (side quest 8): this world's Tectonic biomes include peaks above Y=400. A turbine farm up there produces enormous passive output and never needs fuel.
