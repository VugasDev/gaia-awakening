# Using the Quest Book

## Opening the Book

Press **[** (default keybind) to open the FTB Quests interface. You can rebind this in
**Options → Controls** if it conflicts with another mod.

## Chapter Groups and Tiers

The quests are organised into five tier groups that map directly to the progression tiers:

| Group | Title |
|-------|-------|
| T1 | Tier 1: The Beginning |
| T2 | Tier 2: Brass and Machines |
| T3 | Tier 3: Energy and Automation |
| T4 | Tier 4: Advanced Systems |
| T5 | Tier 5: Endgame |

Within each tier group there are multiple chapters. In Tier 1, for example, you have **First Steps**
(survival), **The Andesite Age** (Create basics), and **Into the Wild** (exploration). Select a chapter
tab on the left side of the book to see its quest map.

## Quest Shape Legend

Each quest in the book is displayed as a shape. The shape tells you what kind of quest it is:

| Shape | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Circle (default) | Main quest — part of the critical progression path |
| Diamond | Optional side quest — extra rewards, not required to advance |
| Gear | Milestone gate — a significant checkpoint in the progression |

In the source data, main quests have no shape set (the chapter default is a circle). Diamond quests carry
`optional: true` and offer rewards for doing extra work. Gear quests mark major gates — for example, the
gear-check quest before the Tier 5 boss encounters, and the final completion milestone.

## Quest Dependencies

Quest icons are connected by lines showing which quests must be completed before the next one unlocks. You
cannot submit a locked quest. Follow the arrows to see the required path — locked quests appear grayed out.

## Completing Quests and Claiming Rewards

Most quests track progress automatically once you craft or collect the required items. A few use manual
**checkmark** tasks — click the checkmark button in the quest panel to confirm you have done the action.

When a quest is ready to claim:

1. Open the quest book (**[**).
2. Click the glowing quest icon.
3. Click **Claim Rewards** in the quest panel.

Rewards include items, XP points, and XP levels. They go directly into your inventory — make sure you have
space, or stand over a surface where items can drop.

## Tips

- **Diamond quests are worth doing.** Optional side quests often give items (magnet upgrades, golden apples,
  ore boosts) that are more valuable than the effort required.
- **Read the quest descriptions.** Each quest has a description and a subtitle that explain the mod mechanic
  being introduced — these are often faster than looking up a wiki.
- **Cross-chapter dependencies exist.** A quest in one chapter can require completion of a quest in a
  different chapter. The connecting lines will indicate this when you hover over locked quests.
