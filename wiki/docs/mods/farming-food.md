# Farming & Food Mods

This category covers two distinct farming systems: **Mystical Agriculture** (crop-based resource generation, a core progression path) and the **Farmer's Delight** family (cooking and food diversity, an optional comfort layer). **Serene Seasons** affects both systems by introducing crop growth restrictions per season.

---

## Mystical Agriculture

**Mystical Agriculture** lets you grow almost any resource as a crop — iron, diamonds, Mekanism's Osmium, AE2's Certus Quartz, and more. Each resource type has its own Essence Seed; you craft the seed, plant it on Essence Farmland, and harvest Essences that convert back into the resource.

Tiers 1-4 cover vanilla resources and are unlocked through the quest book starting at Tier 2. Tier 5 and 6 (modded resources like Osmium and Allthemodium) are gated behind Mekanism and AE2 progress respectively.

### Why this matters for progression

- Mystical Agriculture removes late-game ore scarcity without removing the need for early mining.
- Higher-tier crops require Inferium → Supremium Essence upgrades, each requiring crafting from the tier below.
- Seeds for Mekanism materials (Osmium, Refined Obsidian) are gated so you cannot skip Create/Mekanism processing.

### Mystical Agradditions

**Mystical Agradditions** extends Mystical Agriculture with Tier 6 crops for modded resources — Osmium, Certus Quartz, Compressed Iron, and others depending on what mods are loaded. These are the final resource automation step and are available only at Tier 4-5 in the quest book.

!!! note "Cross-reference"
    Mystical Agriculture Essence materials also appear in [Magic](magic.md) crafting recipes (Ars Nouveau and Apotheosis use elemental Essences). Growing Inferium and refining it to higher tiers feeds both your resource supply and your magic progression.

## Farmer's Delight

**Farmer's Delight** adds a cooking system to Minecraft: you place a Cooking Pot over a fire, add ingredients, and produce meals that provide stacking saturation buffs beyond normal food. It also adds crop variants (tomatoes, rice, cabbage, onions, etc.) found in village chests or grown from seeds.

This is an optional comfort path — food buffs from Farmer's Delight do not block any quest gate, but well-fed characters regenerate health faster and have better stamina for long dungeon runs.

### Farmer's Delight addons

| Mod | Role |
|---|---|
| **Veggies Delight** | More vegetable crops and Farmer's Delight compatible recipes. Adds structures (market stalls) where you can find seeds. |
| **More Delight** | Additional meals, ingredients, and cooking options. |
| **Chef's Delight** | Adds a Cook/Baker Villager profession who trades Farmer's Delight ingredients and recipes. |
| **Create: Garnished** | Bridges Create and Farmer's Delight — adds Create-compatible processing for Farmer's Delight crops (mechanical mixing, pressing). |

## Serene Seasons

**Serene Seasons** adds four seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) that cycle in real time. Each season affects:

- **Crop growth rates** — most crops grow slowly or not at all in winter. Plan your Mystical Agriculture farms with this in mind.
- **Biome appearance** — snow in autumn, coloured leaves, rain frequency changes.
- **Mob spawning** — some mobs are more common in specific seasons.

The current season is visible in the top-left of the F3 debug screen and in the quest book's Farming chapter. A greenhouse (glass-roofed enclosed farm) bypasses the winter growth penalty.
